## üè™ Grocery Store Chains

The Norwegian market is dominated by three main players: **NorgesGruppen**, **Coop**, and **Reitan Retail (Rema 1000)**. Understanding the differences is key to saving money.

### 1. NorgesGruppen
The largest player with the widest range of concepts.
*   **Kiwi:** The "green" discount chain. Focus on low prices, "Bleieavtale" (diaper deal), and freshness guarantee. Usually matches the lowest prices in the market.
*   **Meny:** Full-service supermarket with a huge selection, fresh food counters (meat, fish, cheese). More expensive, but offers high quality and specialty items.
*   **Spar:** Local supermarkets, often good offers on weekly staples (look for "10-kroners marked").
*   **Joker:** Small convenience stores, often open on Sundays (Brustad-bua). expensive for full weekly shops but critical for emergencies.

### 2. Reitan Retail
*   **Rema 1000:** The original discount chain. "Det enkle er ofte det beste" (The simple is often the best). Heavily focuses on their own brand (Prima) and large pack sizes. Often intense price wars with Kiwi.

### 3. Coop
Owned by the members. Dividend (Kj√∏peutbytte) is a unique selling point.
*   **Coop Extra:** Discount chain, competes with Kiwi and Rema 1000. Large selection for a discount store.
*   **Coop Obs:** Hypermarket. Huge bulk packs, non-food items, and weekly "Obs-deals" which can be very cheap.
*   **Coop Mega:** High-end supermarket similar to Meny.
*   **Coop Prix:** Neighborhood store.
*   **Coop Marked/Matkroken:** Smaller local stores.

### 4. Others / Online
*   **Oda:** Online-only grocery store. immense selection, prices comparable to Rema/Kiwi/Extra, delivered to your door. Good for planning and avoiding impulse buys.
*   **Bunnpris:** Known for "self-service" hours in some locations and Sunday open stores.
